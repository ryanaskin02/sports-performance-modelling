# Session-RPE 

- _Use of RPE-Based Training Load in Soccer_ by Impellizzeri et. al. (2004)

This study applied Foster's session-RPE method to football and assessed its correlations with various HR-based methods of determining internal training load. Nineteen young football players were involved, and individual internal loads from 479 training sessions were collected. All indvidual correlations between HR-based training load measures and session RPE were statistically significant. The study concluded that session-RPE can be considered a good indicator of global internal load of football training. 

- _Factors Influencing Perception of Effort (Session Rating of Perceived Exertion) During Elite Soccer Training_ by Gaudino et. al. (2015)

This is from Manchester United's sports science department. It examined which external load variables most influenced sRPE in genuinely elite EPL players. Gaudino et al. demonstrated that a combination of external load variables predicts sRPE better than any individual parameter alone. This shows that internal response is multi-factorial, not driven by a single GPS metric.
